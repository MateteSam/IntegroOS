The Parchments of Truth by Pastor Alex Iheme 21 February 2026Bible: 2 Timothy 2Key Verse: 1Thou therefore, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.
Theme: Be Strong and Courageous
Message After the death of Moses, the man of God, the Lord chose Joshua, Moses’ servant, to lead the children of Israel into the promised land. To prepare him for this great task, God spoke words of encouragement: “Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and of a good courage; be not afraid, neither be thou dismayed: for the LORD thy God is with thee whithersoever thou goest.”
Through obedience to God’s instruction and holding firmly to His promises, Joshua led Israel into thirteen grievous battles and won them all—the first being the battle of Jericho, where the formidable walls fell. In total, he defeated thirty-one kings (Joshua 12:7-24).
In our theme verse, Paul gave Timothy a similar charge: “Thou therefore, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.”
To be strong in grace means to rely not on your own ability, but on the finished work of Christ. The flesh may be weak, but through the grace of Christ, weakness becomes strength. It means holding on to God’s word and standing firm against opposing circumstances that try to nullify or contradict His promises in your life.
Through this grace, we affirm the authority of the name of Jesus over every other name—principalities, powers, dominions, and kingdoms—bringing them into subjection to God’s word.
A believer is a soldier of Christ. He takes advantage of grace to enforce the will of God in his life and in his world, regardless of surrounding circumstances.
Prayers Holy Father, thank You for the saving grace we have in Christ Jesus. I declare that I stand in grace and by grace—not by my abilities, but by Christ’s abilities. I can do all things through Him who strengthens me. Amen!
