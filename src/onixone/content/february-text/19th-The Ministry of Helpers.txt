Theme: The Ministry of Helpers
Message There are those whom the Holy Spirit has endowed with the spiritual gift of helping others, as a ministry for the edification of the body of Christ, the Church (1 Corinthians 12:28).
They have the unique ability to identify others who are struggling and in need. They possess extraordinary compassion, reaching out through words of encouragement, providing support, and rendering assistance to relieve pressure and alleviate suffering.
In the church, they are the helpers of the war and the helpers of the work.
The church is in a battle against the forces of darkness, contending for the souls of men unto eternal life. Evangelical outreaches and gospel propagation projects are launched for this purpose. Though the gospel is given freely, it comes with cost implications. Helpers of the war step in through financial support, relieving the church of burdens and making up for the lack of others.
Helpers of the work assist with planning, administration, management, cleaning, and many other services. This is what Paul meant in 1 Corinthians 3:6: “I have planted, Apollos watered; but God gave the increase.”
Just like Mary Magdalene, Joanna, Susanna, and many others who followed Jesus and ministered to His needs—even to the cross. And like Aaron and Hur, who held up Moses’ hands for victory during the battle with the Amalekites (Exodus 17:12). These ministers of the gospel, anointed to help, strengthen the body of Christ to fulfill its divine mandate.
As the Lord commands, “Go ye into all the world, and make disciples of all nations.” And so they serve!
Prayers Dear Father, thank You for the appointment of helpers toward the building up of the Church. Blessed be Your name—Amen!
