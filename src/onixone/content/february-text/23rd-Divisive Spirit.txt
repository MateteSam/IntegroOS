The Parchments of Truth by Pastor Alex Iheme 23 February 2026Bible: Romans 16Key Verse: 17I urge you, brothers and sisters, to watch out for those who cause divisions and put obstacles in your way that are contrary to the teaching you have learned. Keep away from them.
Theme: Divisive Spirit
Message A divisive person is someone who causes disunity and creates divisions among a group. Sadly, such individuals are also found in the house of God.
But how do you know when someone is expressing honest concerns versus when they are being divisive?
Common traits of divisive people include:
Always finding fault with things done in the church, without stepping in to offer help.
Constantly having “better ideas” and opinions, yet failing to produce fruit in their own lives.
Loving to be heard and advising everyone else, but not living out what they preach.
Being quickly offended and undermining church leadership, leading to disunity and division.
We should not go searching for divisive people in church, but rather concentrate on the teachings of the Word, which is able to correct such behaviors.
Divisiveness is not of God—it is a spirit. The root of divisiveness is selfishness. The Bible says that such people do not serve Jesus Christ but their own belly (Romans 16:18). It is possible for someone to be divisive without realizing it. The Bible provides a way to help such a person: by speaking to them.
Titus 3:10 says, “Warn a divisive person once, and then warn them a second time. After that, have nothing to do with them” (NIV). By warning someone, you bring them back to their senses and make them aware of their disposition. These warnings must be accompanied by prayers, and the final instruction—keeping away—should be rarely applied and only as a measured step.
However, when a brother or sister refuses the instructions and guidance of the church after many warnings, then indeed the church must keep away from them.
Prayer Father, You are building Your church. Thank You for making us obedient to Your will. The gates of hell shall not prevail, in Jesus’ name—Amen!
